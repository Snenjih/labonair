# AI Assistant Overview

Labonair ships with a fully integrated AI assistant that lives alongside your terminal and SFTP sessions. It is not a bolt-on chatbot — it has direct access to your workspace context, can read and write files, execute commands, and track multi-step tasks in real time.

## How It Works

The assistant is built on the [Vercel AI SDK](https://sdk.vercel.ai/) and communicates directly with OpenAI (or any compatible provider) from your machine. There is no Labonair server in the middle — your conversations and API calls are strictly between your Mac and the AI provider.

The assistant is powered by an **agent loop**: it can call tools, inspect results, and continue reasoning across multiple steps until the task is complete or the step limit is reached.

## Bring Your Own Key (BYOK)

Labonair does not bundle any API credentials. You must supply your own OpenAI API key (or a key for a compatible provider such as Ollama or Groq) before the assistant becomes active. See [Setting Up an API Key](./setting-up-api-key) for the setup flow.

## Opening the AI Panel

Toggle the AI panel with `Cmd+Shift+A` or click the AI icon in the left sidebar. The panel floats over the workspace or can be docked, depending on your layout preference.

## What the Assistant Can Do

- **Read files and directories** — without asking for approval
- **Write or create files** — pauses for your explicit approval before writing
- **Run terminal commands** — shows you the exact command and waits for your go-ahead
- **Access your live terminal context** — sees the current working directory and the last 300 lines of your terminal's scrollback buffer at the moment it needs them
- **Manage multi-step tasks** — uses a built-in Todo Strip to track progress visibly

## Session Persistence

Conversations are organized into named **sessions**. Sessions survive app restarts — they are stored locally via `tauri-plugin-store` in `terax-ai-sessions.json` inside your app data directory. No conversation data ever leaves your machine except as part of the API request to your chosen AI provider.
